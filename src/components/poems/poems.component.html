Poem 1

A second is all I need,
A moment is all I wish,
There's a rage burning inside,
There are days all I do is weep,
Your courage was filled with strength,
Yet I saw death overpower you,
I heard fear in the voice of the brave,
Now am here looking at your grave,
The inevitable that I will never understand,
The reality we cannot evade.
A conversation might be extreme,
All I crave is to stare,
All I hate is that you are not here,
There's a void that cannot be filled,
A pain that cannot be felt,
Tear that cannot be wiped,
A parent that cannot be replaced,
Love that can NEVER be replaced.

Poem 2

To do some little good before i die;
To wake some echoes to a loftier theme;
To spend my life's last store of industry;
On thoughts less vain than Youth's discordant dream;
To endow the world's grief with some counter-scheme;
Of logical hope which through all time should lighten;
The burden of men's sorrow and redeem;
Their faces' paleness from the tears that whiten;

To take my place in the world's brotherhood;
As one prepare to suffer all its fate;
To do and be undone for the sake of good;
And conquer rage by giving love for hate;
That were a noble dream, and so to cease;
Scorned by the proud but with the poor at peace.
